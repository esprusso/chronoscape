## Design Context

### Users
Chronoscape is for private personal reflectors who want to log memories, revisit life events, and gradually see the shape of their story on a timeline. Many users are likely new to structured timeline journaling, so the interface should help them begin without feeling like they need to organize their whole life up front.

### Brand Personality
Warm, reflective, intimate. The product should feel private and steady rather than clinical, gamified, or aggressively productivity-driven. It should invite memory work with calm confidence and make users feel that their story is being handled with care.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a literary, tactile visual language with soft warmth and restrained ornament. The interface should feel like a personal archive in progress, not a dashboard. Favor graceful typography, gentle color contrast, privacy-forward framing, and UI that encourages one thoughtful action at a time.

### Design Principles
1. Get users to the first meaningful memory quickly.
2. Keep guidance optional and never nag after dismissal.
3. Treat privacy and emotional sensitivity as part of the product experience.
4. Prefer calm, human language over technical or overly instructional copy.
5. Reveal depth progressively after the first win instead of explaining everything up front.
